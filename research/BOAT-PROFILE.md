## Identity
- **Name:** Juana María **[confirmed]**
- **Type:** *ballenera* — a classic double-ended (doble proa, canoe stern both ends), shallow-draft
  wooden sailing boat developed by Manuel M. Campos for the wide, shallow Río de la Plata. **[confirmed]**
  (Overhead drone video confirms she is pointed at both bow and stern.)
- **Year built:** 1941 **[confirmed — stated in the "La Distancia" on-screen credits]**
- **Home port / waters:** San Isidro, Buenos Aires (Río de la Plata); habitually crosses to
  Colonia del Sacramento, Uruguay. **[confirmed]**
- **Flag:** Argentina. Hull lettered with her name over her **REY** registration
  (Registro Especial de Yates, Prefectura Naval Argentina). The bow shows two stacked lines —
  upper line ~"REY …" / lower line "JUANA MARIA"; exact REY number not fully legible in any photo. **[likely]**

## Design & construction
- **Designer:** **Manuel M. Campos** (1894–1987), the Argentine naval architect who created the
  *ballenera* type (first hull *Nutria*, 1939). The on-screen credit reads "BALLENERA DIBUJO DE
  MANUEL CAMPOS DEL AÑO 1941". **[confirmed]**
  - ⚠️ The owner's "Ocampos" is a mis-remembering of **Campos**. Use "Campos".
- **Builder / shipyard:** **Astillero Agustín Cadenazzi**, Tigre (on the Río Luján).
  **[confirmed — bronze builder's plaque aboard]** — the boat carries a bronze builder's plaque
  (photographed; see `media-web/photos/details/builders-plaque-*`) that legibly reads
  *"JUANA MARÍA / MANUEL M. CAMPOS / DIBUJANTE / AGUSTÍN CADENAZZI / CONSTRUCTOR / TIGRE"*. This
  first-party evidence upgrades the builder from broker-only to confirmed (it had also been the
  Finara listing's claim). Cadenazzi is a verified Tigre yard (later built the ARA Petrel, 1965,
  and Prefectura patrol boats).
  - Note: Campos' principal ballenera builder was generally **José Parodi** (Tigre) — a class-level
    fact, but for Juana María the plaque names **Cadenazzi**. Safe copy: *"built in 1941 at the
    Agustín Cadenazzi shipyard in Tigre."*
- **Originally commissioned by:** Judge ("juez") **Tedín** — of the prominent Salta-origin Tedín
  jurist family (lineage of federal judge Virgilio Mariano Tedín), Buenos Aires elite of the 1940s. **[broker + genealogy]**
- **Hull material:** **Viraró** wood (frames, stringers, planking), the standard Campos ballenera
  spec (with lapacho for keel/stem/sternpost). **[broker + ADAN]**

## Heritage framing (for a "history" section)
- The *ballenera* is Manuel Campos' answer to the shallow Río de la Plata, c.1939: pronounced sheer,
  long keel, V-ended, modest rig, retractable centerboard — seaworthiness and comfort over speed.
  He drew ~25 balleneras; Juana María (1941) is among the **earliest**, built ~2 years after the type's invention.
- Campos' inspirations: Norwegian double-enders (Colin Archer / William Atkin), the traditional
  Buenos Aires whaleboats of the old roadstead, and builder José Parodi (met 1931).
- Same drawing board produced **LEHG II** (Vito Dumas' solo circumnavigation of the three great capes,
  1942–43) and **Gaucho** — Juana María belongs to this celebrated lineage.
- "Built in Tigre" ties her to the Delta's historic boatbuilding hub (40+ yards mid-20th century).
